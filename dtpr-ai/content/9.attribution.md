# Attribution

DTPR for AI is an extension of the [Digital Trust for Places & Routines](https://docs.dtpr.io) standard. This page documents the license terms inherited from DTPR and any additional license terms that apply to content adapted from external taxonomies.

## Research foundations

Where content in the `ai` datachain type is adapted from external research, the source is documented here with its full citation and any additional license terms.

### Risks taxonomy (`ai__risks_mitigation`)

The risk taxonomy in the AI schema is adapted from the **AIAAIC Collaborative, Human-Centred Taxonomy of AI, Algorithmic, and Automation Harms**. AIAAIC's 9 harm types — Autonomy, Physical, Psychological, Reputational, Financial & Business, Human Rights & Civil Liberties, Societal & Cultural, Political & Economic, Environmental — organize the DTPR risk category from the victim's perspective: *what could happen to a person*, not *where the failure originates in the system*.

**Citation**

Abercrombie, G., Benbouzid, D., Giudici, P., Golpayegani, D., Hernandez, J., Noro, P., Pandit, H., Paraschou, E., Pownall, C., Prajapati, J., Sayre, M.A., Sengupta, U., Suriyawongkul, A., Thelot, R., Vei, S., & Waltersdorfer, L. (2024). *A Collaborative, Human-Centred Taxonomy of AI, Algorithmic, and Automation Harms.* arXiv:2407.01294 [cs.LG] (v2, November 2024). https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2407.01294

[Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International (CC BY-SA 4.0)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/). Confirmed on the arXiv paper and the [OECD.AI catalog entry for the AIAAIC Repository](https://oecd.ai/en/catalogue/tools/aiaaic-repository).

**Project**

The living taxonomy and its underlying incident database (1,500+ documented incidents as of mid-2024) are maintained at [aiaaic.org](https://www.aiaaic.org/).

## Contributing back

Gaps or extensions identified while authoring the DTPR risk catalog that are also relevant to AIAAIC's general-purpose taxonomy — for example, harm framings specific to public-space AI deployment — will be proposed back to the [AIAAIC working group](https://www.aiaaic.org/) as upstream contributions, in the spirit of the ShareAlike license.

## Per-element citation

Every element adapted from an external taxonomy also carries its source in the element's `citation` field, retrievable via the MCP `get_element` tool or REST `GET /api/v2/schemas/:version/elements/:element_id`. Per-element citations are the authoritative record; this page is the human-readable summary.
